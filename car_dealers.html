In a market where COE prices and buyer sentiment shift quickly, staying ahead matters. The Car Dealers Dashboard gives you a clear view of market trends — from COE price movements to registration and revalidation patterns — helping you plan smarter. With data-driven insights, you can anticipate demand, align stock levels, and time your promotions for maximum impact. Whether it’s identifying when customers are most likely to renew or when new car interest peaks, this dashboard turns market data into actionable strategy for every dealer.

What does the interest number represent?
  • Numbers represent search interest relative to the highest point on the chart for the given region and time. A value of 100 is the peak popularity for the term. A value of 50 means that the term is half as popular. A score of 0 means that there was not enough data for this term.

  • 2. Understanding each graph
  • Left Graph: Overall Market Elasticity
    The graph shows the overall elasticity of new registrations to COE price for the entire market.
    → New Registration = − 0.02516 × (Weighted COE Premium Month Total) + 46,198.2
    → R² = 0.55 → moderate-to-strong relationship
    → p-value ≈ 0.009 → statistically significant
    - Interpretation: On average, when COE premiums increase, total car registrations fall significantly — about 2.5% fewer registrations for each $100K rise in COE total (approximate linear estimate).
  • Right Graph: Class-Specific Elasticity (Category A vs B)
    Category A: Steeper downward slope
    - Category A (Mass-Market Cars): High elasticity — buyers are more price-sensitive. A 10% increase in COE could reduce new registrations by 8–10%.

    - Category B (Luxury Cars): Low elasticity — buyers are less affected. A 10% COE increase might only reduce registrations by 3–5%.
